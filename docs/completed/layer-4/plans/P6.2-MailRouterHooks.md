## Test Descriptions

### Test 1: TestMailRouter_RouteWithSecurity_Validation

**Given**: A MailRouter with SecurityService injected and mail from outer to inner boundary  
**When**: RouteWithSecurity is called with the mail  
**Then**: SecurityService.ValidateAndSanitize is called before routing, EXTERNAL taint added  
**Expected Result**: Mail validated, taints modified, then routed to target service  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L290 (Mail-only transitions), L1303 (ValidateAndSanitize)

### Test 2: TestMailRouter_BoundaryValidation_ForbiddenTransition

**Given**: A MailRouter with mail containing forbidden taints `["PII", "SECRET"]` attempting inner to outer transition  
**When**: RouteWithSecurity is called with the mail  
**Then**: Transition is blocked or taints are stripped based on policy, violation reported if blocked  
**Expected Result**: Mail either modified with stripped taints or taint_violation emitted to dead-letter  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L284 (auto-strip or block), L286 (taint_violation event)

### Test 3: TestMailRouter_AllowedOnExit_Stripping

**Given**: A MailRouter with allowedOnExit policy `["TOOL_OUTPUT"]` and mail with taints `["PII", "TOOL_OUTPUT", "SECRET"]`  
**When**: RouteWithSecurity is called for mail leaving runtime (to outer boundary)  
**Then**: Only TOOL_OUTPUT taint preserved, PII and SECRET stripped  
**Expected Result**: Mail.Metadata.Taints contains only `["TOOL_OUTPUT"]` after routing  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L1317 (allowedOnExit enforcement), L210-213 (allowedOnExit policy)

### Test 4: TestMailRouter_ViolationRouting_DeadLetter

**Given**: A MailRouter with mail that violates boundary policy (e.g., outer to inner with SECRET taint)  
**When**: RouteWithSecurity is called and violation occurs  
**Then**: taint_violation Mail created and routed to sys:observability dead-letter queue  
**Expected Result**: Violation mail contains source/target boundaries, forbidden taints, timestamp  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L286 (taint_violation to dead-letter), L170 (MailTypeTaintViolation)
