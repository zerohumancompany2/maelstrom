## Test Descriptions

### Test 1: TestEnforceStreamBoundary_PerChunk

**Given**: Multiple StreamChunks sent sequentially with different taints  
**When**: EnforceStreamBoundary is called on each chunk independently  
**Then**: Each chunk is processed without dependency on previous chunks  
**Expected Result**: No state carried between chunks; each chunk sanitized based on its own taints  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L299 (sanitization is per-chunk stateless)

### Test 2: TestEnforceStreamBoundary_Latency

**Given**: A StreamChunk of typical size (e.g., 1KB content)  
**When**: EnforceStreamBoundary is called and processing time is measured  
**Then**: Processing completes in <50ms  
**Expected Result**: Measured latency <50ms under normal load conditions  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L299 (latency stays <50ms)

### Test 3: TestEnforceStreamBoundary_NoBuffer

**Given**: A stream of 10 chunks sent through EnforceStreamBoundary  
**When**: Chunks are processed and emitted  
**Then**: Each chunk is emitted immediately after processing without accumulation  
**Expected Result**: Chunks arrive at output channel in real-time, no batch buffering  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L300 (no buffering unless chart explicitly requests)

### Test 4: TestEnforceStreamBoundary_AllowedOnExit

**Given**: A StreamChunk with taints `["PII", "TOOL_OUTPUT", "INNER_ONLY"]` and allowedOnExit policy `["TOOL_OUTPUT"]`  
**When**: EnforceStreamBoundary is called with the policy  
**Then**: Only TOOL_OUTPUT taint remains in output chunk  
**Expected Result**: Chunk.Taints contains only `["TOOL_OUTPUT"]`, forbidden taints stripped  
**Spec Reference**: arch-v1.md L1317 (SHALL enforce allowedOnExit on streams)
